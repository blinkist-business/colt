§ 01
Context

The challenge

Colt is building a high-performing organisation through one of the hardest 18 months in its history, a cyber incident in 2025, a 2026 spend freeze, and a workforce reshape running through September. Learning has to earn its place by tying directly to performance, retention and revenue.

The leadership framework is being designed. What's missing is content and coaching the business will actually engage with.

01

6,000 LinkedIn Learning licences, ~5–10% engagement

The platform is paid for but barely used. As a revenue-led L&D conversation, that's a budget already spent without business case to defend it.

02

A technical culture that resists soft skills

Engineers happily learn the next technology or AI tool. Storytelling, leading change, manager craft, those land harder. You need formats designed to break through, not another e-learning library.

03

Coaching can't scale 1:1 across 6,000 people

You're designing a coaching framework with layered targets: ~60 CEO-1 leaders, a time-poor CEO-2 group, and ~750 line managers in CEO-3. 1:1 coaching doesn't math at that scale.

04

Every euro needs a business case

Post-incident, only revenue-generating spend gets through. L&D needs to show retention impact, capability lift, and a hard ROI, not "nice to have."

The business case

A 14× return, and the maths behind it

Conservative estimates for a 500-seat rollout giving access to ~750 line managers, plus 60-leader coaching cohorts. Benchmarked against Blinkist customer data.

1. Business impact
8,640skill applications / yr
Managers applying frameworks and insights directly to live Colt customer, commercial and team challenges.
500 active learners × 24 books / yr × 72% application rate. Better managers → better teams → better retention numbers.
2. Retention
+0.5%retention uplift · ~2.5 high performers retained / yr
A modest 0.5-point retention lift across the 500-seat manager population, material through a year where morale is under pressure.
500 seats × 0.5% = 2.5 high performers retained. Avoided replacement cost: 1.5× avg. fully-loaded salary (€80K) × 2.5 = €300K.
3. Time saved
90+ daysof L&D curation / yr
Your team stops curating, sourcing and stitching content, and focuses on the leadership framework itself.
~3 days saved per programme × 30 programmes = 90 days ≈ €30–50K of internal effort.
4. Money saved
€300–330Kannually
€240K attrition avoided · €30–50K L&D effort · €20–30K consolidated from underused vendors.
Optional cohort coaching: 90-min sessions at ~€140/person vs. €200–400/hr for 1:1 = 53–76% cheaper than equivalent external coaching.
Overall ROI
14×, a 1,364% return

~€300K of value annually versus a €22K Year 1 investment. Less than the fully-loaded cost of one mid-level hire, and before counting the impact of 8,000+ skill applications across the manager population.

Conservative, floor-level estimates designed to survive scrutiny in a CHRO and CFO conversation. Compounding across 750 managers makes the true return materially higher.

§ 05
Integration

Sits inside your learning stack, not alongside it

LinkedIn Learning isn't working at scale and renewal is a decision point. Blinkist is built to plug into your existing stack, so you consolidate spend rather than add another tool.

Option 1

Single Sign-On (SSO)

Learners access Blinkist with their existing Colt credentials, no extra logins, no IT overhead, fast for distributed teams.

Option 2

SCORM packages

Curated summaries and pathways packaged as SCORM and dropped into your LMS, assignable, trackable, reportable as native learning objects.

Option 3

API completion data

Completion data flows back via API, Colt's people team can report against the leadership framework without manual exports.

We work with your IT team to configure SSO and reporting in the first 2 weeks, so Blinkist is live before the Q3 coaching cohorts begin, not after.
